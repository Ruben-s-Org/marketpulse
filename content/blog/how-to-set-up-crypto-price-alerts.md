# How to Set Up Crypto Price Alerts (Step-by-Step Guide)

Crypto markets never sleep. Prices can swing 10% while you're at dinner. Setting up price alerts means you're always informed — without staring at charts 24/7.

This guide shows you how to set up free crypto price alerts that actually work, so you never miss a trade again.

## Why You Need Crypto Price Alerts

The crypto market trades 24/7/365 across global exchanges. Unlike stocks, there's no closing bell. Key reasons to set up alerts:

- **Catch breakouts** — Get notified when Bitcoin breaks above resistance
- **Manage risk** — Know immediately when prices drop to your stop-loss level
- **Time entries** — Buy the dip when prices hit your target
- **Stay informed** — Track portfolio moves without constant chart-watching

## Method 2: CoinGecko

CoinGecko offers free price alerts through their mobile app:

1. Download the CoinGecko app (iOS/Android)
2. Create a free account
3. Navigate to any coin's page
4. Tap "Price Alert"
5. Set your target price and direction

**Pros:** Free, covers 10,000+ coins
**Cons:** Mobile app only, no stock/forex alerts, limited alert types

## Method 3: TradingView

TradingView has robust alerting for crypto and stocks:

1. Open any crypto chart on TradingView
2. Right-click the price level → "Add Alert"
3. Configure conditions (price crossing, indicator-based, etc.)
4. Choose notification method

**Pros:** Advanced conditions, indicator-based alerts
**Cons:** Free tier limited to 5 active alerts, paid plans start at $14.95/mo

## Method 4: Exchange-Native Alerts

Most exchanges offer built-in alerts:

### Coinbase
- Open the Coinbase app
- Go to any asset → "Set Price Alert"
- Enter your target price

### Binance
- Open Binance app → Markets
- Select a trading pair
- Tap the alert bell icon
- Set your conditions

**Pros:** Direct trading integration
**Cons:** Only works for assets on that exchange, limited customization

## Alert Strategies for Crypto Traders

### The Breakout Hunter
Set alerts at key resistance levels. When Bitcoin has been consolidating under $72,000 for weeks, set an alert at $72,500 to catch the breakout.

### The Dip Buyer
Set staggered buy alerts at support levels:
- BTC at $65,000 → Buy 25%
- BTC at $60,000 → Buy 25%
- BTC at $55,000 → Buy 50%

### The Portfolio Guardian
Set alerts at -10% from your entry price on every holding. This gives you time to evaluate whether to hold or cut losses.

### The Altcoin Scout
Set percentage-change alerts on mid-cap altcoins. A sudden 15% move often signals news or momentum worth investigating.

## Best Practices for Crypto Alerts

1. **Don't over-alert** — Too many notifications cause alert fatigue. Focus on actionable levels.
2. **Use round numbers** — Markets often react at psychological levels ($50K, $100K).
3. **Combine with volume** — A price alert is more meaningful when confirmed by volume.
4. **Review weekly** — Remove stale alerts and adjust levels as the market moves.
5. **Use multiple timeframes** — Set short-term trading alerts and long-term investment alerts.
